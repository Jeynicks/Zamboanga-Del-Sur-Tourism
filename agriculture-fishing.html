Agriculture and Fishing Industry

Overview

Agriculture and fishing are the twin pillars of the Zamboanga del Sur economy. These industries support the majority of the provincial population and contribute significantly to the local food supply and export earnings.

Coconut Industry

Coconut is the dominant agricultural crop, with large plantations spread across the lowland and coastal areas. Copra is the primary product, and the province is a significant copra supplier to processing facilities in Mindanao.

Rice and Corn

Rice is cultivated in the irrigated lowland areas, particularly in the municipalities of Labangan, Kumalarang, and Tukuran. Corn is also widely grown and serves as both food and animal feed.

Fishing

Fishing is a critical livelihood for coastal communities. Artisanal fishermen use traditional methods to catch reef fish, tuna, sardines, and squid in the rich waters of Illana Bay. Commercial fishing operations also work the deeper waters of the Moro Gulf.

Seaweed and Aquaculture

Seaweed farming has grown considerably in coastal municipalities, providing income for small-scale farmers. Fish ponds, crab fattening, and other aquaculture activities are also practiced.

Challenges and Support

Farmers and fisherfolk face challenges including low farm-gate prices, post-harvest losses, climate variability, and limited access to credit and markets. The Department of Agriculture and local government units implement support programs.

Key Facts

  • Top Crop: Coconut/Copra
  • Rice Production: Significant in lowland areas
  • Fishing Grounds: Illana Bay, Moro Gulf
  • Seaweed Farming: Growing aquaculture industry
  • Support Programs: DA and BFAR assistance programs
